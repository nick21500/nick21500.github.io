Please read before opening any additional emails(Opens New Window)

Please be aware that emails are being transmitted from other WCTC user accounts- both Student and Staff- that are not legitimate emails.

Some emails are about financial offers, some promise money or ask for purchases to be made, some promote a job offer that was not initiated both from other companies and potentially WCTC Jobs, and other possible misleading attempts to get a response from the person receiving the email including asking them to login with their username and password. 

While it is common to use email for many legitimate things- this announcement is to ask for your help to recognize the non-legitimate WCTC emails and stop them from continuing. They are spam emails from other users whose accounts are compromised.

Things you should look for to identify a spam or phishing email: 

  • The emails are not signed by a person or the user they are being sent from.
  • There is no information indicating a direct person/team to follow up with about the information in the email.
  • The information is about things you have not initiated with that department or person.
  • They do not address you by name in the beginning of the email.
  • They have a link that makes it sound like you need to do something, verify something, or may get money to check it out.
  • It sounds too good to be true- but not outlandish.
  • The email may be from a known peer you work with or have class with and you may already trust – but have not talked about the information in the email.

What you can do:

  • Verify emails with the person who sent the email to confirm they did actually send it- preferably with a phone call or face to face- don’t reply to the email.
  • Do not click on any links that ask for your WCTC credentials or other types of login verifications or ask for your password or any other personal info.
  • Do not agree to give out any banking or account information, or make financial purchases without verifying the source request.
  • Do not verify anyone else’s information in email or on the phone to others without that person’s consent.
  • Do not give personal information or pertinent personnel information out on the phone or in email if you already do not have a known communication with them.
  • Call the company to verify any job offers directly (do not just google the company) and do not send out resumes or personal employment information until you have verified with that company that the job is legitimate.
  • Delete any emails immediately- do not click on any links- and reset your WCTC password immediately. Then, go to MyServiceSuite and create a case – listing the nature of the email, and who you got it from. Do not forward emails to anyone.

To change your password, please use the ‘Manage my password’ tool, which is on the myWCTC portal on the right side of the home page.

The instructions are also attached to this post.

The above measures will help in stopping the transmission of further spam emails, but also enlist you as our best protection against these issues.

If you need additional help with what to do or help resetting your password, please contact the WCTC Service Desk as soon as possible. 

Thank you for your immediate attention to this issue.

Attend the Lifelong Learning Workshops(Opens New Window)

Learn how to use evidence-based learning, motivation, and productivity principles to learn and excel at WCTC. Attend one or more of the four 30-minute Lifelong Learning Workshops: 

 

  • Crush Bad Study Habits: Find out what scientific research has discovered about how we best learn. Deep processing (THINK), distributed, interleaved study (SPACE), and regular retrieval practice (TEST) need to be part of your study plan.

 

  • Take Note: Effective note taking can help you THINK deeply and make lasting memories. Learn how to annotate your textbook, how to make two-column notes in class, and how to employ dual coding and study effectively with notes.

 

  • Don't Eat the Marshmallow: When embarking on challenging educational and career goals, you need to know how to get and stay motivated. Learn how GRIT, a GROWTH MINDSET, and SELF-CONTROL can help you excel as a learner.

 

  • Getting Things Done: Excelling as a learner requires discipline and hard work. You can’t afford to procrastinate. Learn how you can use time management principles grounded in research to stay focused, organized and productive.
